I went to college at Case Western Reserve University (B.S. 2007) and grad school at Columbia University (M.S. 2010), studying electrical engineering at both. I've had a few engineering jobs throughout the years.

I have many diverse interests, almost too many to keep track of. I like rock climbing, surfing, sailing, hiking, biking, photography, woodworking, metalworking, electronics, programming,...

I really like to create things. That is probably a reason why I became an engineer. I get to create things for a living.

I enjoy hardware and software design. I really like working at a low level. Gaining a deep understanding of whatever I am working on is a great thrill for me. Given that, I tend to gravitate torwards analog hardware and low level software. That said, it is also very fun to work at a high level and build a complete system. For that, software is generally more fun because the debugging tools are better.

I first knew I wanted to be an engineer when I joined my high school's FIRST robotics club (team 63) and got to work with some smart engineers from GE volunteering their time. FIRST robotics was a great experience. It was fun to see all the hard work pay off with a functional machine that did what you told it to do.

I think I would still be an engineer without FIRST. I had already discovered programming and was intrigued that one could get paid for writing programs, which was so much fun. FIRST served to cement my career plans.

I spend much of my free time rock climbing and thinking about rock climbing. I got hooked in Februrary, 2008. Ever since then, I have been climbing nonstop. I used to mostly boulder in a gym and on the Sonoma coast. Then, I started a progression from toproping outside to sport climbing and finally to trad climbing in the summer of 2009. My first trad climbing experience was with my friend Brian at Seneca Rocks in West Virginia. We did a five pitch route called "Old Ladies Route" up to the summit. I later bought some gear and started climbing in the Gunks while in grad school.

I used to go surfing and sailing a lot. I was a member of the Cal Sailing Club and would drive down to Berkeley on the weekends to go sailing. I eventually stopped sailing and surfing much because it was hard to get the weather and tides to cooperate when I had free time. When I discovered rock climbing, the urge to drive and hour or more just to check the weather disappeard. I will eventually pick both back up again, but I think I will need to live very close to the ocean to do so.
