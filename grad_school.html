I graduated in May of 2010 with a Master of Science degree in Electrical Engineering from Columbia University.

I had an interesting time at Columbia. Moving from California to New York City was an immense change. I went from living amongst the rolling hills and vineyards of Sonoma County to the concrete jungle of West Harlem. Perhaps the most difficult adjustment was getting used to garbage and litter strewn about everywhere.

The best classes that I took were:

  • Genomic Sequencing Lecture and Lab with Dr. Ju.

    This was an interesting set of classes in the chemical engineering department. The first class was a lecture series about all the different sequencing techniques and technologies. In the second class, I got to work in a genetics lab and perform research involving genetic sequencing

    • Midterm report about my efforts to search for blood-born bacteria in heart arteries using genetic sequencing techniques.
    • Final report about various techniques to detect single nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs).
  • Music Signal Processing with Dr. Ellis

    This was a very interesting class that taught various DSP techniques to analyze and synthesize sound.

    • Voder presentation that I gave during the first few weeks of class.
    • A paper on note recognition and a Java/Processing program I made to recognize and display the notes in a piece of classical music.
    • A paper on singing synthesis and a Pure Data patch used to synthesize an opera voice.
    • Final project report on a vocoder patch I made in Pure Data.
  • Modern Dislay Science and Technology

    This was a cool class taught by Prof. Kymissis. In it we learned about pretty much every major display technology and how our brains and eyes work to provide vision. We also worked in a lab to build several displays using utilizing various technologies. We built a dot matrix OLED and LCD display from scratch and drove them using an Arduino. We also made electroluminescent displays and even built a Nixie tube clock.
